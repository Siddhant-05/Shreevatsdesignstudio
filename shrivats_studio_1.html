The Studio

Built on the geometry of
ancient India

Shrivats Studio was born from a singular belief — that the most enduring architecture speaks two languages simultaneously: the timeless grammar of sacred Indian geometry, and the refined vocabulary of contemporary luxury.

We draw from the step-wells of Rajasthan, the carved stone screens of Mughal courtyards, the proportional systems of Vastu Shastra, and the brutal honesty of raw material — weaving them into spaces that feel both ancient and entirely of this moment.

Our Belief

The Philosophy
of Space

"Every space we create is a
geometry of intention — drawn
first in silence, then in stone."

We believe architecture is not merely built — it is remembered. Spaces should carry the intelligence of ancient proportion systems, the warmth of handcrafted material, and the restraint of a practiced eye.

Vastu

Spatial orientation rooted in the cardinal geometry of the cosmos — every threshold, every axis, every opening informed by ancient science.

Shilpa

The artisan tradition of Indian craftsmanship — stone, wood, metal — worked with hands that understand time as a building material.

Mandala

The sacred plan. Architecture conceived as a mandala — from the centre outward, from the sacred inward, always in conversation with the infinite.

Akasha

Space as the fifth element. The negative spaces, courtyards, and voids are designed with as much intention as the walls that define them.

Kala

The art of timing. We understand light as it moves through a building across seasons, designing shadows that are as considered as the structure itself.

Dhvani

The resonance of material. Stone sings differently from timber. Brass from terracotta. We compose spaces the way a musician composes sound.

The Method

Five Stages
of Creation

01
Shravana
Deep listening. Before any line is drawn, we spend time understanding how a client inhabits space — their rituals, rhythms, and the silences between.
02
Chintan
Contemplation. We move through site at different hours of the day, different seasons of the year, mapping light, wind, and the land's own geometry.
03
Rachana
Creation. From initial mandala sketches to technical precision — every drawing is a conversation between the sacred and the structural.
04
Nirman
Construction. We work directly with master craftsmen — stone carvers, metal smiths, lime plasterers — who carry living traditions in their hands.
05
Samarpan
Offering. The handover of space is ceremonial. We present not just a building, but a complete sensory world — curated down to the last threshold detail.
